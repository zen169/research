Chapter 01

A Hybrid World Is the New Normal

Work has changed for good. The events of recent years inspired a decade of digital transformation in just months. Priorities for IT leaders were accelerated, abandoned, and adjusted as organizations scrambled to enable remote working at scale.

Questions shifted from "how do we enable remote work?" to "what will the return to work look like?" With initiative lists turned on their heads, many leaders are still working to balance competing priorities: cloud migration, security upgrades, enhanced user experience, and the constant evolution of the modern workplace.

Chapter 02

What's So Great About the Office?

Places of work can be expensive—rent, equipment, maintenance, and other overheads add up. Yet only a tiny fraction of organizations are willing to give up the office entirely.

Almost half of IT leaders cite collaboration as the primary benefit of returning to the office, while concerns about employee morale remain the biggest drawback.

"If the primary purpose of an organization's space is to accommodate specific moments of collaboration rather than individual work, should 80 percent of the office be devoted to collaboration rooms?"

Jane Doe

Partner, Strategic Consulting

Chapter 03

The Appeal of Distributed Working

Remote working is wildly popular. Over half of organizations surveyed listed employee morale as the primary benefit of providing remote options.

There are other benefits too—reduced overheads, productivity boosts, and an increasingly competitive advantage in attracting top talent in a rapidly shifting labor market.

Why Working from Anywhere Is Hard

The biggest obstacle facing IT teams isn't productivity or morale—it's managing risk and compliance. With reduced visibility into remote endpoints, organizations are exposed to greater security threats than ever before.

Chapter 04

Managing Risk in the Dark

IT leaders face the reality that a large percentage of the workforce will remain distributed. Many are quickly realizing they lack visibility into endpoints, connectivity, and application performance.

Chapter 05

Who Owns What?

The transition to working from anywhere has increased the chance of employees using multiple devices. Questions about management and ownership have become more frequent in the era of hybrid working.

Most organizations are opting to maintain as much management and control as possible over the devices employees use to work, reflecting a shift away from permissive BYOD policies.

Summary

Key Takeaways

The overwhelming majority of organizations will manage some kind of hybrid blend. Although collaboration is harder, staying remote offers huge advantages in cost and employee happiness—making it a competitive advantage for employers that channel the outcomes effectively.

  • Hybrid working is the future — nearly 80% of organizations will adopt some form of blended policy.
  • Collaboration remains the #1 reason to come back to the office, cited by 43% of leaders.
  • Employee morale is the top benefit of remote work — but also the top drawback of office work.
  • Risk management is the #1 concern — only 38% have full visibility into distributed devices.
  • Technology is the biggest barrier — 54% say tools and technologies are the hardest challenge to solve.
